Ask CFPB

A resource for questions about financial products

We have a database of clear, impartial answers to hundreds of financial questions. You can use the API to search and retrieve answers, many of which are also available in Spanish.

Answer topics covered include:

  • Auto loans
  • Bank accounts and services
  • Credit cards
  • Credit reports and scores
  • Debt collection
  • Families and money
  • Money transfers
  • Mortgages
  • Payday loans
  • Prepaid cards
  • Student loans

Usage

The API is a read-only resource driven by search, and it delivers data as json. You can search for questions and answers by terms and receive a list of question/answer pairs and their URL at the CFPB.

Requests follow this URL pattern:

  • https://www.consumerfinance.gov/ask-cfpb/search/json/?q=[SEARCH TERMS]

You'll get back a json response that includes a list of results. The payload for the search term "tuition" would look like this, but with more result entries:

{
    query: "tuition",
    suggestion: null,
    result_query: "tuition",
    results: [
        {
            url: "http://www.consumerfinance.gov/ask-cfpb/what-is-a-tuition-payment-plan-en-563/ ",
            text: "Tuition payment plans, also called tuition installment plans, are short-term (12 months or less) payment plans that split your college bills into equal monthly payments. Tuition installment plans can be an alternative to student loans if you can afford to pay tuition, just not in a lump sum at the start of the semester or quarter. These payment plans do not generally charge interest, but they may have up-front fees. What is a tuition payment plan? ",
            question: "What is a tuition payment plan? "
        }
    ]

Notes

  • The API is a public resource and does not require authentication, but heavy or abusive usage could result in the imposition of restrictions on its use.
